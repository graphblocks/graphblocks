# GraphBlocks Governance

GraphBlocks uses maintainer consensus for technical and project decisions.

## Roles

Contributors participate through issues, reviews, documentation, code, and
conformance fixtures. Maintainers may merge changes, manage releases, moderate
project spaces, and make security decisions. Repeated, sustained contributions
and sound judgment are the basis for adding maintainers; inactivity or conduct
violations may lead to removal by the remaining maintainers.

## Decisions

Routine changes proceed through pull-request review. Public contract changes,
breaking compatibility changes, and new conformance profiles require a design
issue or architecture decision record and review from at least two maintainers
when two are available. Maintainers seek consensus; when consensus cannot be
reached, the maintainers responsible for the affected subsystem decide and
record the rationale.

Security response may be handled privately until coordinated disclosure is
safe. Conduct reports are handled under [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## Releases

Maintainers approve releases after the claimed profiles pass their TCK and
acceptance gates. Release notes must identify breaking contracts, migrations,
known limitations, and the exact evidence revision. No individual may
unilaterally broaden a conformance claim without executable evidence.

This governance model may evolve through the same public design process.
